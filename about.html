How it's calculated

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, widely regarded as one of the most accurate. Multiplying by an activity factor gives your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) — roughly the calories to maintain weight.

Macros convert calories using 4 kcal/g for protein and carbs, and 9 kcal/g for fat.

This is a general estimate for healthy adults, not medical or dietary advice. Consult a professional before making significant changes.
